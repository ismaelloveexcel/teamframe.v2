# Outreach Sequences

Two proven entry points. Use both in parallel.

---

## Sequence A — Job Posting Interception (highest conversion)

**Why this works:** The company has publicly admitted they need HR help, already have budget allocated, and are facing a 2–3 month recruiting gap. You are not convincing them they have a problem — you are offering a faster path to the outcome they already want.

**How to find targets:**
- LinkedIn Jobs → search "HR Manager remote" / "People Operations remote" / "Head of People remote"
- Filter: UK companies, posted in last 30 days
- Check company size — target 15–60 employees
- Look up the founder or CEO on LinkedIn (they are usually the hiring manager at this size)

---

### Message A1 — LinkedIn DM (founder of company with active HR job posting)

> Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed [Company] is hiring for HR. Before you spend 3 months recruiting and £40–50K on a salary, I run HR functions for growing startups on a fractional basis — fully set up in 2 weeks, structured system included.
>
> Worth a 20-minute call to see if it fits where you are right now?

**Character count:** Under 300. Do not add more.

---

### Message A2 — Email version (if you have their email)

**Subject:** Re: your HR hire at [Company]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I came across [Company]'s HR Manager role and wanted to reach out directly.
>
> I run HR functions for startups at your stage — structured system set up in 2 weeks, ongoing fractional support from there. A fraction of the cost of a full-time hire, and no 3-month recruiting process.
>
> Would a quick call be useful? Happy to be direct about whether it's a fit.
>
> [Your name]

---

### Follow-up (5 days after no reply)

> Hi [Name], just circling back on this — happy to send a quick overview if easier. No pressure either way.

One follow-up only. Do not chase beyond that.

## Sequence B — Cold Outreach to Recently Funded Startups

**Why this works:** Companies that just raised seed or Series A have budget, are scaling fast, and the pressure to build operational infrastructure is high. Investors often push for it.

**How to find targets:**
- Crunchbase: filter UK companies, raised seed/Series A in last 6 months, 15–50 employees
- LinkedIn: search "People Operations" OR "HR" in company size 11–50, filter by "No HR person" (look at team page)
- Tech news sources: Tech.eu, Sifted, The Stack for UK startup funding announcements

---

### Message B1 — LinkedIn DM (founder of recently funded startup)

> Hi [Name],
>
> Congrats on the [round] raise. Quick question — as you scale the team, do you have an HR structure in place or is it still founder-managed?
>
> I run HR functions for funded startups at your stage. Happy to share how it works if relevant.

**Note:** Personalise the opening with the funding round name/amount if you know it.

---

### Message B2 — Email version

**Subject:** HR setup for [Company] post-raise

> Hi [Name],
>
> Saw [Company]'s recent raise — congratulations.
>
> As you scale the team, one of the first things that breaks is clarity around who owns what and how new hires are structured. I run the HR function for startups at your stage — structured org setup in 2 weeks, then ongoing fractional support.
>
> Worth 20 minutes to see if the timing is right?
>
> [Your name]

## Objection Responses (keep these ready)

**"We're too small for this"**
> "That's actually the best time — setting this up at 20 people costs a fraction of fixing it at 50. Most founders I speak to waited too long."

**"We already use [tool]"**
> "Tools are only as good as the person maintaining them. What I bring is the person behind the system, not just the system itself."

**"We'll handle it ourselves for now"**
> "Totally understand. When you reach the point where you're spending 5+ hours a week on people decisions, feel free to come back — that's usually when it makes sense."

**"Can we do a free trial?"**
> "I don't do free trials, but the setup call is free and I'll tell you honestly whether this is the right fit for your stage."

**"Can we pay per hour?"**
> "I work on retainer only — it keeps the relationship clean and the work consistent. The packages are structured to give you predictable cost."

## Weekly Outreach Rhythm

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday | Identify 5–8 new targets (job postings + funded companies) |
| Tuesday–Wednesday | Send personalised messages to each target |
| Thursday | Follow up on messages sent 5 days ago |
| Friday | Log all responses in objection-log.md |

Target: 15–20 messages sent per week in active outreach phase.

---

## What to Track

In your target spreadsheet (columns):
- Company name
- Employee count
- Trigger (job posting / funding / referral)
- Contact name
- LinkedIn URL
- Date first contacted
- Response (yes / no / follow up / no reply)
- Outcome (call booked / declined / future follow-up)

One row per company. Update it same day as any contact or response.
