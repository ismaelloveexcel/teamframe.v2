# Offer Overview

## The Offer (Plain English)

> "I run your HR function for you using a structured system — so your roles are documented, your org is clear, and nothing falls through the cracks when you hire."

---

## How to Position This in Conversation

Most founders come to this service after a bad hire. The bad hire exposed that there was no structure, no documented role, no ownership clarity. The company bent around the wrong person instead of staying aligned with the mission.

The correct pitch is not "you need better HR admin." It is:

> "Most founders I speak to come to me after a costly people mistake. The mistake exposed that there was no structure behind the decision. I help you build that structure so the next hire lands correctly — and so you can see problems before they cost you."

Lead with the hiring pain. The documentation, compliance tracking, and org clarity are what you build to prevent it. They are the mechanism, not the pitch.

---

## What Is Being Sold

This is a **managed HR operating system service**. It is not software. It is not consultancy.

The buyer receives:
- A functioning HR structure for their organisation
- Ongoing maintenance of that structure as the company grows
- A named person who handles people operations on their behalf
- A system that keeps org clarity, documentation, and compliance tracking current
- A First Look Report at setup — what a fresh set of eyes sees that the internal team has stopped seeing

---

## What TeamFrame Is

TeamFrame is the **internal execution system** used to deliver this service.

It is not the product being sold. It is the tool used to run the engagement.

TeamFrame handles:
- Mapping org structure (positions, reporting lines, departments)
- Tracking assignments (who is in which role, since when)
- Monitoring compliance status (missing JDs, unsigned documents, incomplete records)
- Surfacing org health signals (vacancies, gaps, overdue items)
- Maintaining a full history of org changes

The client does not interact with TeamFrame directly as a product. They receive the output — a maintained, documented, and structured HR function.

---

## Who This Is For

**Primary target:** Companies with 20–50 employees that are either:
- Actively hiring (adding 1–3 people per month)
- Structurally unstable (unclear ownership, undocumented roles, founder handling all HR decisions)
- Post-funding (seed or Series A in the last 12 months)

**They do not have:**
- A dedicated HR person
- A formal HR system
- Documented job descriptions for most roles
- A clear org structure anyone outside the founding team could read

**They do have:**
- A founder spending time on people decisions
- Real compliance risk they have not yet noticed
- Hiring velocity that is outpacing their current coordination capacity

---

## Core Promise

1. **Clarity** — every role is documented, every owner is named, every gap is visible
2. **Structure** — the org runs from a system, not from memory and group chats
3. **Reduced founder load** — people decisions do not require the founder every time
4. **No gaps when hiring** — new roles are set up properly before the person starts, not after

---

## What This Is Not

- Not a payroll system
- Not a recruitment agency
- Not an employment law firm
- Not a complex HRIS with dozens of modules
- Not unlimited HR consultancy

It is the lightweight operational layer between "we track everything in spreadsheets" and "we need a full enterprise HR department."
