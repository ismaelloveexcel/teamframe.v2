These are all strong directions, but they differ a lot in value density, build complexity, and how defensible the product is. If you’re trying to pick one that is both high-impact and clean to execute as a system, here’s the structured breakdown.

🧠 Evaluation Framework (what actually matters)

For a startup HR tool, the winning idea is usually:

Immediate pain (weekly/daily use)
Low data dependency (works even with partial data)
Clear visual output (one screen = insight)
Actionability (not just reporting)
Natural expansion path (becomes platform later)
⚖️ Option Comparison
A. Onboarding & Compliance Tracker
Strengths
Very concrete and easy to understand
High-frequency operational use
Easy to build MVP (checklist engine + status tracking)
Naturally ties into your existing TeamFrame signals/actions model
Weaknesses
Slightly narrow scope (HR ops only)
Can become “task list tool” unless expanded
Verdict

Strong MVP candidate, but not the most strategic long-term wedge

B. Equity & Vesting Dashboard
Strengths
High value in startups (founder + early employees care deeply)
Financially sensitive → strong retention driver
Clear structured data model
Weaknesses
Requires precision (legal/finance expectations are high)
Harder to simulate meaningfully without real cap table data
Less connected to org structure / operations layer
Verdict

High value but high risk + narrow audience

C. People Risk Heatmap ⭐ (BEST OPTION)
Strengths
Combines everything you already built:
org structure
compliance state
leave/offboarding
reporting depth
Extremely visual (perfect for a single dashboard)
High executive value (founder/CEO tool)
Naturally supports your computeUIState architecture
Expandable into a “people intelligence layer”
Weaknesses
Slightly abstract at first (needs good UX framing)
Verdict

Best overall choice — highest leverage + aligns perfectly with your simulation engine

D. Hire Plan / Budget Simulation
Strengths
Strategic planning value
Finance + HR intersection = strong product category
Weaknesses
Requires assumptions (salary, runway, forecasting logic)
Less “truth-based”, more modeling-heavy
Can drift into spreadsheet territory
Verdict

Powerful later-stage feature, not ideal MVP core

👉 Build: C. People Risk Heatmap

Uses your existing deterministic compute engine directly
